Headaches affect nearly everyone at some point. While medication helps, acupressure offers a drug-free alternative you can practice anywhere. This guide covers the most effective pressure points for headache relief.

## Top 5 Acupressure Points for Headaches

### 1. LI4 (Hegu) - Union Valley

**Location:** In the webbing between thumb and index finger

**How to find it:** Bring thumb and index finger together. The point is in the highest spot of the muscle that bulges.

**Technique:**
1. Use opposite thumb to apply firm, circular pressure
2. Press for 1-2 minutes per hand
3. Breathe deeply while applying pressure

**Best for:** Frontal headaches, tension headaches

### 2. GB20 (Fengchi) - Wind Pool

**Location:** Base of skull, in the hollow between neck muscles

**How to find it:** Place fingers at the base of skull, slide them toward the spine until you feel two depressions

**Technique:**
1. Use both thumbs simultaneously
2. Apply upward pressure toward the base of skull
3. Hold for 1-2 minutes while breathing deeply

**Best for:** Tension headaches, neck-related headaches

### 3. Yintang - Third Eye Point

**Location:** Between eyebrows, where bridge of nose meets forehead

**Technique:**
1. Use index or middle finger
2. Apply gentle circular pressure
3. Continue for 1-2 minutes

**Best for:** Stress-related headaches, sinus headaches

### 4. GB21 (Jianjing) - Shoulder Well

**Location:** Midpoint between neck base and outer shoulder

**Technique:**
1. Use opposite hand to pinch the muscle
2. Apply firm, downward pressure
3. Hold for 30-60 seconds, release, repeat

**Best for:** Tension headaches, shoulder tension headaches

**Caution:** Avoid during pregnancy

### 5. LV3 (Taichong) - Great Rushing

**Location:** Top of foot, between big toe and second toe bones

**How to find it:** Run finger from between toes up foot until you feel a depression

**Technique:**
1. Use thumb to apply firm pressure
2. Press for 1-2 minutes per foot
3. Angle pressure slightly toward ankle

**Best for:** Migraines, stress headaches

## Complete Headache Relief Routine

For best results, follow this 10-minute sequence:

1. **Start with LI4** (both hands, 2 minutes each)
2. **Move to GB20** (both sides simultaneously, 2 minutes)
3. **Press Yintang** (between eyebrows, 2 minutes)
4. **Finish with LV3** (both feet, 2 minutes each)

Perform in a quiet space, breathing deeply throughout.

## When to Use Acupressure

**During headache onset:** Apply pressure at first sign of headache for best results.

**Preventively:** Use daily if you have frequent headaches.

**Alongside medication:** Acupressure can reduce medication needs but consult your doctor before changing medications.

## Safety Tips

- Avoid excessive pressure (moderate, comfortable pressure works best)
- Don't use acupressure on broken skin or wounds
- Some points (like GB21) should be avoided during pregnancy
- Seek medical care for severe, sudden, or concerning headaches

## The Bottom Line

Acupressure offers a simple, effective tool for headache relief you can use anywhere, anytime. While it may not replace medical treatment for chronic headaches, many people find it provides meaningful relief without side effects.
